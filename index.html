Marble bust of the Roman Emperor Marcus Aurelius in a fringed cloak.

Here's a time line of Marcus Aurelius' life:

  • 26 Apr 121 CE - 17 Mar 180 CE
    Life of Marcus Aurelius.
  • Apr 26th, 121 CE
    Born as Marcus Annius Verus III in Rome, into a wealthy, prominent family, in Rome, Italy.
  • 132 CE
    Marcus Aurelius is introduced to philosophy by his teacher Diognetus.
  • 138 CE
    Marcus Aurelius adopted by Antoninus Pius as successor.
  • 140 CE
    Aurelius became emissary or leader of the senate —a position he would hold twice in his life.
  • 145 CE
    Marcus Aurelius marries Faustina, daughter of Antoninus Pius.
  • 161 CE
    His father dies; Marcus rose to power and was officially known as ‘Marcus Aurelius Antoninus Augustus’.
  • 161 CE - c. 166 CE
    Parthian Wars with Rome; Lucius Verus commands Rome's forces in the field.
  • 7 Mar 161 CE - 17 Mar 180 CE
    Reign of Roman Emperor Marcus Aurelius.
  • 9 Mar 161 CE - Mar 169 CE
    Marcus Aurelius rules with Lucius Verus.
  • c. 162 CE - c. 166 CE
    Marcus Aurelius persecutes new sect of Christianity.
  • Oct 166 CE
    Lucius Verus and Marcus Aurelius celebrate a shared triumph, both are hailed as Pater Patriae.
  • 168 CE
    Germanic tribes cross the Danube into the Roman Empire.
  • 169 CE
    Lucius Verus dies.
  • Mar 169 CE - 177 CE
    Marcus Aurelius rules alone.
  • 170 CE - 180 CE
    Marcus Aurelius leads campaigns against the Germanic tribes; writes his famous Meditations, a philosophical text.
  • 175 CE
    Marcus Aurelius tours eastern provinces of empire; his wife Faustina dies.
  • c. 176 CE
    A huge bronze statue of Marcus Aurelius on horseback is erected in Rome.
  • 177 CE - 17 Mar 180 CE
    Marcus Aurelius rules with his son Commodus.
  • Mar 17, 180 CE
    Marcus Aurelius dies (aged 58) in Sirmium, Pannonia; his son Commodus succeeds his father as the emperor.
